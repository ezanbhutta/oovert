01

The premise

Most branding is camouflage.

Safe palettes. Borrowed language. Logos engineered to offend no one — and remembered by no one. It is expensive invisibility, billed monthly.

We practice the opposite.

o·vert / oʊˈvɛːt / adjective

  1. Done or shown openly.
  2. Plainly apparent; not secret.
  3. See also: the companies that win.

Visibility isn’t vanity. It’s economics.

Distinct brands command price premiums, survive bad quarters, and make sales calls shorter. The unmistakable get chosen first.

03

The practice

We do few things. On purpose.

  1. 01

    Excavation

    Weeks of interviews, audits, and awkward questions before a single pixel. We dig until we hit the one truth competitors can’t copy. That’s the foundation. Everything else is decoration.

  2. 02

    Position

    One sentence that picks a fight. If it could belong to the company next door, it isn’t a position — it’s a hiding place.

  3. 03

    Identity

    The truth, made visible. Name, mark, voice, type, motion — a system recognized before it’s read.

  4. 04

    Arsenal

    Guidelines, tooling, templates, governance. The unglamorous machinery that keeps a brand sharp long after the launch party.

Engagements from US$120K. Never more than three clients at once.
